## Getting Started

### System Requirements

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Modern web browser (Chrome 90+, Firefox 88+, Safari 14+, Edge 90+)
- Stable internet connection (5 Mbps or higher recommended)
- Webcam and microphone (for full interview experience)
- 4GB RAM minimum

**Recommended:**
- Chrome or Edge browser for best performance
- 10 Mbps internet connection
- HD webcam
- Noise-canceling microphone or headset
- Quiet environment for practice

The platform requires the following permissions:
- **Camera**: To record your interview session
- **Microphone**: To capture your spoken responses
- **Notifications**: To receive interview updates (optional)

You'll be prompted to grant these permissions when starting your first interview.

## Starting an Interview

### Step 1: Choose Your Interview Mode

The platform offers three interview modes:

#### 1. Resume-Based Interview
- Questions tailored to your uploaded resume
- Focuses on your specific experience and skills
- Best for: Preparing for roles matching your background

#### 2. Job Description-Based Interview
- Questions based on a specific job posting

- Best for: Preparing for a specific job application

1. **Select Job Role**: Choose from available categories

1. Grant camera and microphone permissions when prompted

4. Adjust lighting and positioning

## Interview Modes

### Resume-Based Interview

**How It Works:**
1. Upload your resume
2. AI analyzes your experience, skills, and projects
3. Generates 5-10 personalized questions
4. Questions probe deeper into your specific background

**Example Questions:**
- "I see you worked on a microservices project at Tech Corp. Can you explain the architecture decisions you made?"
- "Your resume mentions experience with AWS. Which services did you use and why?"

**Best For:**
- Practicing for roles that match your experience
- Preparing to discuss specific projects
- Getting feedback on how to present your background

---

### Job Description-Based Interview

**How It Works:**
1. Paste the job description (up to 5000 characters)
2. AI identifies key requirements and skills
3. Generates role-specific questions
4. May include coding challenges for technical roles

**Example Questions:**
- "This role requires experience with Kubernetes. Describe your container orchestration experience."
- "The JD mentions leading cross-functional teams. Tell me about a time you did this."

**Best For:**
- Preparing for a specific job application
- Understanding what skills to emphasize
- Practicing answers aligned with job requirements

---

### General Interview

**How It Works:**
1. Select your target role category
2. AI generates standard questions for that role
3. Covers technical, behavioral, and situational topics
4. Balanced difficulty progression

**Example Questions:**
- "Explain the difference between REST and GraphQL APIs"
- "Describe a time you had to meet a tight deadline"
- "How do you handle disagreements with team members?"

**Best For:**
- General interview preparation
- Building confidence with common questions
- Practicing without a specific target role

## During the Interview

### The Interview Interface

**Main Components:**

1. **3D Avatar Interviewer** (Top Center)
   - Animated AI interviewer
   - Displays expressions and gestures
   - Provides visual engagement

2. **Question Display** (Center)
   - Current question text
   - Question category badge
   - Question number (e.g., "Question 3 of 7")

3. **Answer Input Area** (Bottom)
   - Text area for typing answers
   - Voice recording button
   - Word count display

4. **Timer** (Top Right)
   - Countdown for current question
   - Turns yellow at 30 seconds remaining
   - Turns red at 10 seconds remaining

5. **Progress Bar** (Top)
   - Shows interview completion percentage
   - Updates after each question

6. **Mentor Mode Toggle** (Top Left)
   - Enable/disable structured guidance
   - Shows CAR framework prompts when active

### Answering Questions

#### Option 1: Type Your Answer
1. Click in the answer text area
2. Type your response
3. Use the word counter to gauge length (aim for 100-200 words)
4. Click **"Submit Answer"** when ready

#### Option 2: Voice Response
1. Click the **microphone icon** to start recording
2. Speak clearly and at a moderate pace
3. Your speech is transcribed in real-time
4. Review the transcription
5. Edit if needed
6. Click **"Submit Answer"**

**Voice Recording Tips:**
- Speak clearly and avoid filler words ("um", "uh")
- Pause briefly between sentences
- Check transcription accuracy before submitting
- Use a quiet environment for best results

### Mentor Mode

When enabled, Mentor Mode guides you through the CAR framework:

**Context**: Set the scene
- What was the situation?
- What was your role?
- What challenges did you face?

**Action**: Describe what you did
- What specific steps did you take?
- What skills did you use?
- How did you approach the problem?

**Result**: Share the outcome
- What was the impact?
- What did you learn?
- What metrics demonstrate success?

The AI evaluates your answer against this framework and provides specific feedback on each component.

### Coding Challenges (Technical Roles)

For technical roles, you may encounter coding challenges:

1. **Challenge Description**: Read the problem statement carefully
2. **Test Cases**: Review example inputs and outputs
3. **Code Editor**: Write your solution using Monaco Editor
   - Syntax highlighting
   - Auto-completion
   - Multiple language support (Python, JavaScript, Java, C++)
4. **Language Selection**: Choose your preferred programming language
5. **Submit Code**: Click "Run Code" to test, then "Submit" when ready

**Coding Tips:**
- Read the problem twice before coding
- Consider edge cases
- Write clean, readable code
- Add comments to explain your logic
- Test with provided examples first

### Real-Time Feedback

After submitting each answer, you'll receive:

1. **Score** (0-100): Numerical rating of your answer
2. **Feedback**: AI-generated evaluation
3. **Strengths**: What you did well
4. **Improvements**: Areas to enhance
5. **Follow-Up Question** (sometimes): Deeper probe based on your answer

**Score Interpretation:**
- **90-100**: Excellent - Comprehensive, well-structured answer
- **80-89**: Very Good - Strong answer with minor improvements possible
- **70-79**: Good - Solid answer, some areas to develop
- **60-69**: Fair - Adequate but needs more depth or clarity
- **Below 60**: Needs Improvement - Missing key elements or unclear

### Completing the Interview

The interview ends when:
- All questions are answered
- You click "End Interview" (if available)
- Time limit is reached (if applicable)

After completion:
1. Session is automatically saved
2. Performance report is generated (10-15 seconds)
3. You're redirected to the Results page
4. Confetti celebration animation plays! 🎉

## Understanding Your Results

### Performance Report Overview

Your comprehensive performance report includes:

#### 1. Overall Score
- Aggregate score across all questions
- Displayed as a percentage and visual gauge
- Color-coded: Green (80+), Yellow (60-79), Red (<60)

#### 2. Category Scores

**Technical Score**
- Evaluates technical knowledge and accuracy
- Based on technical questions answered
- Shows depth of expertise

**Behavioral Score**
- Assesses soft skills and situational responses
- Evaluates use of frameworks (STAR, CAR)
- Measures communication effectiveness

**Communication Score**
- Analyzes clarity and articulation
- Considers word choice and structure
- Evaluates confidence and professionalism

#### 3. Word Count Metrics

**Average Words Per Answer**: Indicates response length
- **Ideal Range**: 100-200 words
- **Too Short** (<75 words): May lack detail
- **Too Long** (>250 words): May be unfocused

**Total Words**: Overall verbosity
**Per Question Breakdown**: Chart showing variation

#### 4. Sentiment Analysis

**Confidence Level** (0-100%)
- Measures assertiveness and certainty
- Based on language patterns and tone
- Higher is generally better (aim for 70-85%)

**Clarity Score** (0-100%)
- Evaluates how understandable your answers are
- Considers structure and coherence
- Target: 80%+

**Professionalism Rating** (0-100%)
- Assesses appropriate language and tone
- Checks for informal or inappropriate content
- Maintain 85%+

#### 5. Strengths

List of what you did well:
- "Strong technical explanations with specific examples"
- "Excellent use of the STAR method in behavioral questions"
- "Clear and concise communication"

#### 6. Weaknesses

Areas needing improvement:
- "Could provide more quantifiable results"
- "Some answers lacked specific examples"
- "Consider structuring responses more clearly"

#### 7. Recommendations

Actionable advice for improvement:
- "Practice the CAR framework for behavioral questions"
- "Add metrics to demonstrate impact (e.g., '20% performance improvement')"
- "Research common algorithms for technical questions"

#### 8. CAR Framework Score (if Mentor Mode was used)

- Evaluates adherence to Context-Action-Result structure
- Shows breakdown by component
- Provides specific feedback on each element

### Session Recording

Access your recorded interview:
1. Click **"View Recording"** on the results page
2. Video player with synchronized transcript
3. Playback controls (play, pause, seek, speed)
4. Timestamp navigation

**Uses:**
- Review your body language and presentation

- Practice self-evaluation
- Track improvement over time

### Downloading Your Report

1. Click **"Download Report"** (PDF format)

## Tips for Success

### Before the Interview

**Preparation:**
1. **Update Your Resume**: Ensure it reflects your latest experience
2. **Research the Role**: Understand common questions for your target position
3. **Review Fundamentals**: Brush up on technical concepts
4. **Practice STAR/CAR**: Familiarize yourself with structured answering
5. **Set Up Your Space**: Quiet room, good lighting, professional background

**Technical Setup:**
1. Test camera and microphone beforehand
2. Close unnecessary browser tabs and applications
3. Ensure stable internet connection
4. Use headphones to reduce echo
5. Have a backup device ready

### During the Interview

**Best Practices:**

1. **Take Your Time**
   - Read questions carefully
   - Think before answering (5-10 seconds is fine)
   - Don't rush through responses

2. **Structure Your Answers**
   - Use STAR or CAR framework
   - Start with a clear opening statement
   - Provide specific examples
   - End with results or learnings

3. **Be Specific**
   - Use concrete examples from your experience
   - Include metrics and numbers when possible
   - Avoid vague generalizations
   - Name technologies, tools, and methodologies

4. **Manage Time**
   - Monitor the timer
   - Aim for 1.5-2 minutes per answer (100-200 words)
   - Don't let time expire without submitting
   - Practice conciseness

5. **Stay Professional**
   - Maintain good posture
   - Make "eye contact" with the camera
   - Speak clearly and at a moderate pace
   - Avoid filler words and slang

6. **Use Mentor Mode**
   - Enable for structured guidance
   - Follow the CAR prompts
   - Learn the framework through practice
   - Disable once comfortable

### After the Interview

**Review and Reflect:**

1. **Analyze Your Report**
   - Read all feedback carefully
   - Note patterns in strengths and weaknesses
   - Identify specific areas to improve

2. **Watch Your Recording**
   - Observe body language
   - Listen for verbal tics
   - Note areas of discomfort or hesitation
   - Celebrate improvements

3. **Take Action**
   - Research topics you struggled with
   - Practice weak areas
   - Implement recommendations
   - Schedule your next session

4. **Track Progress**
   - Compare scores across sessions
   - Monitor improvement trends
   - Adjust practice focus as needed

### General Interview Tips

**Technical Questions:**
- Explain your thought process aloud
- Break down complex problems
- Consider edge cases
- Discuss trade-offs in your solutions
- Mention scalability and performance

**Behavioral Questions:**
- Always use real examples
- Focus on YOUR actions (not "we", but "I")
- Quantify results when possible
- Show learning and growth
- Be honest about challenges

**Coding Challenges:**
- Clarify requirements before coding
- Think aloud while solving
- Write clean, readable code
- Test your solution
- Explain time and space complexity

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues and Solutions

#### Camera/Microphone Not Working

**Problem**: Browser can't access camera or microphone

**Solutions:**
1. Check browser permissions:
   - Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Site Settings → Camera/Microphone
   - Firefox: Preferences → Privacy & Security → Permissions
2. Ensure no other application is using the devices
3. Try a different browser
4. Restart your browser
5. Check system privacy settings (Windows/Mac)

#### Poor Audio Transcription

**Problem**: Voice responses are transcribed incorrectly

**Solutions:**
1. Speak more slowly and clearly
2. Reduce background noise
3. Use a better microphone or headset
4. Move closer to the microphone
5. Switch to typing if issues persist

#### Session Not Saving

**Problem**: Interview progress is lost

**Solutions:**
1. Check internet connection
2. Don't close the browser during the interview
3. Ensure cookies are enabled
4. Try a different browser
5. Contact support if issue persists

#### Slow Performance

**Problem**: Platform is laggy or unresponsive

**Solutions:**
1. Close unnecessary browser tabs
2. Clear browser cache and cookies
3. Disable browser extensions
4. Check internet speed (minimum 5 Mbps)
5. Try a different browser (Chrome recommended)
6. Restart your computer

#### Can't Upload Resume

**Problem**: Resume upload fails

**Solutions:**
1. Check file format (PDF, DOC, DOCX only)
2. Ensure file size is under 5MB
3. Try converting to PDF
4. Check for file corruption
5. Try a different file

#### Questions Not Loading

**Problem**: Interview questions don't appear

**Solutions:**
1. Refresh the page
2. Check internet connection
3. Clear browser cache
4. Try incognito/private mode
5. Contact support

## FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: Is my data secure?**
A: Yes. We use industry-standard encryption for all data. Passwords are hashed, and personal information is never shared with third parties. See our Privacy Policy for details.

**Q: How long are sessions saved?**
A: All completed sessions are saved indefinitely. You can access your session history anytime from your dashboard.

**Q: Can I delete my account?**
A: Yes. Go to Settings → Account → Delete Account. Note that this action is irreversible.

**Q: Is there a mobile app?**
A: Currently, the platform is web-based. A mobile app is planned for future release.

**Q: How many interviews can I do?**
A: Unlimited! Practice as much as you want.

### Interview Questions

**Q: How are questions generated?**
A: Questions are generated by Google Gemini AI based on your resume, job description, or role category. Each session has unique questions.

**Q: Can I skip questions?**
A: No, all questions must be answered to complete the session. However, you can end the interview early if needed.

**Q: How long does an interview take?**
A: Typically 20-40 minutes, depending on the number of questions (usually 5-10) and your response time.

**Q: Can I pause an interview?**
A: Not currently. Plan to complete the interview in one sitting.

**Q: Will I get the same questions again?**
A: No. The AI avoids repeating questions within the same session and tries to vary questions across sessions.

### Scoring Questions

**Q: How is my score calculated?**
A: Scores are based on multiple factors: answer completeness, relevance, use of examples, technical accuracy, communication clarity, and structure.

**Q: What's a good score?**
A: 80+ is excellent, 70-79 is good, 60-69 is fair. Scores improve with practice.

**Q: Why did I get a low score?**
A: Check the feedback for specific reasons. Common issues: lack of examples, vague answers, poor structure, or missing key points.

**Q: Can I retake an interview?**
A: Yes! Start a new session anytime. Each interview is independent.

**Q: Does the leaderboard affect my job prospects?**
A: No. The leaderboard is for motivation and self-improvement only. It's not shared with employers.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Which browsers are supported?**
A: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge (latest versions). Chrome is recommended for best performance.

**Q: Do I need to install anything?**
A: No. The platform is entirely web-based.

**Q: Can I use it offline?**
A: No. An internet connection is required for AI processing and real-time features.

**Q: What if my internet disconnects?**
A: The session may be lost. Ensure a stable connection before starting. Auto-save features are in development.

**Q: Can I use it on a tablet?**
A: Yes, but desktop is recommended for the best experience, especially for coding challenges.

### Resume & Job Description Questions

**Q: Is my resume stored permanently?**
A: Yes, until you delete it. You can update or remove your resume anytime from your profile.

**Q: Can I upload multiple resumes?**
A: Currently, only one resume per account. You can replace it anytime.

**Q: What if my resume has formatting issues?**
A: Use a simple, ATS-friendly format. Avoid images, complex tables, or unusual fonts.

**Q: Can I edit the job description after starting?**
A: No. You'll need to start a new session with the updated JD.

**Q: Does the AI share my resume with anyone?**
A: No. Your resume is private and used only for generating personalized questions.

### Coding Challenge Questions

**Q: Which programming languages are supported?**
A: Python, JavaScript, Java, and C++. More languages coming soon.

**Q: Is my code executed?**
A: Yes, in a secure sandbox environment. Your code cannot access external resources or cause harm.

**Q: What if my code doesn't run?**
A: Check for syntax errors. The AI will still provide feedback on your approach even if the code doesn't execute.

**Q: Can I use external libraries?**
A: Only standard libraries are available. No external packages or imports.

**Q: How is code quality evaluated?**
A: The AI assesses correctness, efficiency, readability, best practices, and problem-solving approach.
